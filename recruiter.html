Before you open the resume

Here's my two cents: I care a lot about impact. I'm not chasing work just because it sounds impressive on paper. I'm looking for the kind of work where the stakes are real, the learning curve is steep, and the output actually matters.

I've always been pulled toward technology not just because it is intellectually exciting, but because I believe it can change who gets access, who gets heard, and who gets lifted. That belief is still underneath everything I build.

Right now I'm in the MS in Artificial Intelligence program at Northeastern, and I also serve as a Teaching Assistant for the graduate NLP course. One thing I'm genuinely proud of is that I did not take NLP here first and still earned that role on pure skill. To me, that says something important about how I prepare, how I learn, and how I show up when trust has to be earned. I'm also taking Actionable Interpretability — a PhD-level seminar — sitting alongside doctoral researchers and holding my own. I don't just take the path of least resistance.

I've also done multiple internships, multiple research projects, leadership work as a GSG(Graduate Student Government) Senator, and I've shipped deployed projects people can actually use. So when you look at me, I don't want you to see someone who only fits one category. I want you to see someone who can build, research, teach, communicate, and take ownership.

The simplest way I can put it: throw me into the ocean and I'll figure out how to swim, read the current, and still bring something back to shore. I like being challenged. I like steep ramps. I like environments that expect a lot — because I expect a lot from myself too.

If that sounds like the kind of person your team values, start with the resume. The full portfolio has the longer version — projects, research, deployed work, and the details behind how I think.
