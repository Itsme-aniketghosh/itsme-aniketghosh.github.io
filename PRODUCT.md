# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

AI/ML hiring managers, ML research collaborators, academic researchers in interpretable ML and computational biology, potential co-op/internship employers at tier-1 startups and labs. These are expert-level readers: they notice when claims are vague, they respect precision, and they have seen every generic engineer portfolio in existence. The site needs to be worth their attention in the first thirty seconds.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio and professional identity for Aniket Ghosh, an AI/ML engineer specializing in medical AI, mechanistic interpretability, and drug-development pipelines. The site exists to attract high-stakes opportunities: industry ML roles, research collaborations, and academic partnerships where both technical difficulty and real-world consequences are high. Success means the right person reads it and wants to reach out.

## Brand Personality

Sharp, serious, grounded. No softness. The work speaks; the site does not oversell. Authority comes from specificity, not adjectives.

## Anti-references

- **Flashy creative-agency portfolio**: full-screen video intros, particle effects, cursor trails, excessive motion, Awwwards-chasing theatrics. Decorative at the expense of content.
- **Generic SaaS / tech startup**: gradient hero, hero-metric cards, teal or purple palette, testimonial strips, stock illustrations, "We help you build faster" copywriting energy.
- **Academic CV dump**: wall-of-text, no visual hierarchy, publication lists in plain text, zero interaction, resume-in-HTML form.
- **Hype-driven engineer portfolio**: "10x developer" phrasing, GitHub activity widgets, badge collections, neon-on-black aesthetic, Discord link in the nav.

## Design Principles

1. **Specificity is the authority.** Replace every superlative with a precise fact. A GPA, a tool name, a paper title, a drug target carries more weight than "passionate" or "experienced" ever will.
2. **Stakes over features.** Frame work in terms of consequence, not capability. The through-line is: being wrong has consequences. Every project description should make clear why the problem matters, not just what was built.
3. **Every element earns its presence.** No section fills space. No animation runs without a job. No sentence restates the one above it. If removing something makes the page clearer, remove it.
4. **Restraint as decision, not absence.** Minimal decoration is a deliberate choice. The site is dense with substance and sparse with ornament. Quiet surfaces make the content louder.
5. **The narrow path between four failure modes.** Not flashy. Not generic SaaS. Not a CV dump. Not engineer hype. The design exists in the precise space where none of those descriptions apply.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Already implemented in the codebase: `prefers-reduced-motion`, `:focus-visible` on all interactive elements, semantic HTML, and contrast targets. Maintain this baseline across all future changes. No time-limited interactions on critical content.
