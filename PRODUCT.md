# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and researchers at AI labs and ML-heavy product teams, evaluating Aniket Ghosh for Spring 2027 full-time ML/AI roles and co-ops. They arrive from a resume link or LinkedIn, mostly on desktop, and decide within a minute whether to keep reading. A secondary audience is peers and collaborators checking research and projects.

## Product Purpose

Convert a visit into an interview. The site itself is the proof of engineering craft: an AI/ML engineer who ships interpretable, consequence-aware ML should have a portfolio whose details (physics, typography, motion) hold up to scrutiny. Success = time-on-page, resume downloads, and "Talk to My AI Twin" engagement.

## Brand Personality

Stoic, precise, quietly confident. Editorial broadsheet meets engineering lab: large serif declarations, monospace instrument labels, near-black paper. The Marcus Aurelius quote on the ID card back is the voice: discipline over flash.

## Anti-references

- Neon-gradient "developer portfolio" templates (purple/cyan glows, particle backgrounds).
- SaaS landing-page scaffolding: icon cards, gradient text, hero metrics.
- Linktree-style minimal pages that show no craft.

## Design Principles

1. **The site is the resume.** Every detail is evidence of engineering judgment; nothing decorative that couldn't be defended in a code review.
2. **One physical metaphor, obsessively executed.** The hanging ID card (physics, hardware, materials) is the centerpiece; new ideas serve it rather than compete with it.
3. **Monochrome until something is alive.** The palette is warm cream on near-black; color is reserved exclusively for live signals (availability, current roles, running systems).
4. **Motion is physics, not decoration.** Things fall, swing, and settle; they don't bounce or sparkle. Reduced-motion users get a complete, static equivalent.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG AA contrast for body text; muted gray reserved for short labels.
- `prefers-reduced-motion` fully honored (static rope, no drop, no shake).
- Keyboard: visible focus rings, ARIA labels on interactive card elements.
