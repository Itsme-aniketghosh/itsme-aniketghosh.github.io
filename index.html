Introduction

About Me

My path into AI started with contrasts. Growing up, I traveled with my father — an SAP consultant at IBM — through cities like Chicago, Bonn, and Zurich. At the same time, I spent seven years teaching mathematics and computer science to underprivileged students through The Hope Foundation. That gap between what technology could do and who it actually reached became the question I've been trying to answer ever since.

I graduated with a B.Tech in Computer Science & Business Systems from the Institute of Engineering & Management, Kolkata — ranking 2nd in a department of 180+ students with a 9.3/10 GPA and receiving the Director's Award. In Fall 2025, I joined Northeastern University's Khoury College for my MS in Artificial Intelligence with a 15% merit scholarship, focusing on the Machine Learning concentration.

Currently, I'm a Teaching Assistant for the graduate NLP course under Professor Amir Tahmasebi, where I teach PyTorch and ML/DL labs. My coursework spans Foundations of AI, Algorithms, Interpretable AI, and Applied Programming for AI. Outside class, I'm deep into multi-agent systems — building autonomous trading simulations and benchmarking link prediction methods on biomedical knowledge graphs.
