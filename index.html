About Me

My path into AI started with contrasts. Growing up, I traveled with my father, an SAP consultant at IBM, through cities like Chicago, Bonn, and Zurich. At the same time, I spent seven years teaching mathematics and computer science to underprivileged students through The Hope Foundation. That gap between what technology could do and who it actually reached became the question I have been trying to answer ever since.

I graduated with a B.Tech in Computer Science and Business Systems from the Institute of Engineering and Management, Kolkata, ranking 2nd in a department of 180+ students with a 4/4 GPA and receiving the Director's Award. In Fall 2025, I joined Northeastern University's Khoury College for my MS in Artificial Intelligence with a 15% merit scholarship, focusing on the Machine Learning concentration.

I finished TAing the graduate NLP course under Professor Amir Tahmasebi, teaching PyTorch and ML/DL labs, and currently serve as GSG Senator representing graduate students in university governance. I work at Varosync, Inc., a computational drug development startup building an AI engine that simulates a drug's 24-hour pharmacological performance to predict safety and efficacy in clinical trials.
